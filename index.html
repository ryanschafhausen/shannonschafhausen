About Shannon

Shannon Schafhausen is a Licensed Professional Counselor in Colorado. She received her Master of Arts in Counseling from the University of Northern Colorado in 2007 and has practiced since graduating. She has diverse experience in the mental health field and previously worked as a Certified Addictions Counselor. Shannon has worked in mental health centers, EAP services, outpatient practices, hospitals, and county jails.

Shannon is trained in EMDR, which she sees as a highly effective treatment to resolve several presenting problems, including trauma. She draws on Dialectical Behavior Therapy, Cognitive Behavior Therapy, Acceptance and Commitment Therapy, and other evidence-based practices. Shannon primarily focuses on strength-based counseling, supporting individuals with tools to function in a healthy way and meet their goals.

Approach & Methods

Care is tailored to each person and grounded in evidence-based, strength-focused practice.

  • EMDR — Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing, especially effective for trauma.
  • DBT — Dialectical Behavior Therapy for emotion regulation and distress tolerance.
  • CBT — Cognitive Behavior Therapy for changing unhelpful thought and behavior patterns.
  • ACT — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy for values-driven, flexible living.

Frequently Asked Questions

What can I expect from the first session?

The first session is a chance to get to know one another, talk through what brought you in, and begin shaping goals for our work together. There's no pressure — it's a conversation, and you set the pace.

Do you offer telehealth?

Yes. Secure video sessions are available to clients located anywhere in Colorado.

What is EMDR?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is a structured, well-researched therapy that helps the brain process distressing or traumatic memories so they feel less overwhelming.
